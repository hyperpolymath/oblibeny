== What this is, and how it is designed to work

*What it is.* A short, dated, jointly-signed snapshot of what we can _honestly
and verifiably_ claim about the Oblíbený toolchain at one exact commit. Nothing
here is marketing and nothing is a promise about the future — both of those live
in the README. This file is the receipt.

*How it is designed to work.* Three moving parts make it trustworthy:

. *Ground truth, not memory.* Every green claim below was produced by _running
  the tool in the session that wrote this file_ — `dune build`, `dune test`, the
  golden-path interpreter run, the escape-hatch grep, the file-presence checks,
  and `zig ast-check`. Where a status doc, the `Justfile`, or a memory said
  otherwise, the live run wins and we flag the contradiction. *Where a check
  could NOT be run in this session (no `idris2`), we say so explicitly and do
  NOT borrow its result.*
. *A frozen anchor.* The file names the exact commit SHA, branch, UTC timestamp,
  and toolchain (see <<Verifiable anchor>>), so "true" always means "true _at
  this point_". Move the SHA and the file becomes a draft until re-run.
. *A real signature.* It is landed by a *signed git commit*; that signature over
  this content at the anchored SHA is what makes the affirmation tamper-evident
  and attributable — not the prose alone.

*We are fallible.* We can be wrong, stale, or simply mistaken about our own
work. This file is our best honest belief, not a proof of its own correctness.
Treat it as a falsifiable claim, not gospel.

== The epistemic contract (read this before you trust _or_ attack)

This document records our *best joint belief* at the timestamp below. It is
*not a guarantee of correctness.* We may be wrong. We may have missed something
— a stale doc, a check we could not run, a stub we forgot was load-bearing.

*No intentional overclaim.* That is the only guarantee here: we have not
_knowingly_ inflated anything. Where something was run-green-in-this-session we
say so; where it is documented-and-CI-gated-but-not-run-here we say _that_
(distinctly); where it is an explicit stub or an external-dependency blocker, we
name it.

*Standing invitation to refute.* You are _invited_ to bulldoze any claim here.
The fair form of the attack is: (1) read this file at the stamped commit;
(2) reproduce (or fail to reproduce) the checks in <<reproduce>>; (3) _then_
tell us where the discrepancy is, against the artefact as it stood at this
moment, so we can justify it or concede it on the record. An attack that skips
(1)–(2) is attacking a strawman of a different date or environment.

=== The honest nuance you must not lose

A green `dune build` / `dune test` proves *the OCaml toolchain compiles and the
conformance suite passes as written*. The escape-hatch grep proves *the ABI proof
source contains no escape-hatch tokens*. Together they do NOT prove:

* *that the Idris2 ABI proofs type-check at this commit.* `idris2` was *absent in
  this session*, so the proof MUST (`idris2 --build src/abi/oblibeny-abi.ipkg`)
  was *not executed here*. We carry the "genuine total proofs since PR #51, CI-gated
  in PR #52" claim forward as a *documented and CI-gated* fact we did *not*
  re-verify locally. A clean escape-hatch grep is necessary-but-not-sufficient:
  it shows no `believe_me`/`postulate` in the source, not that the proofs close.
  Re-run with `idris2` to upgrade this from "documented" to "affirmed-live".
* *that the crypto FFI links or that `obli-pkg verify` is sound.* The Zig FFI
  *links* only where `liboqs` is installed (absent here; not attempted), and
  `obli-pkg`'s verify path is an *explicit MVP stub* with a placeholder payload
  (`TODO(security)` at `ffi/zig/src/obli-pkg.zig:377`). ast-check passing is not
  linking, and linking is not a verified signature scheme.
* *that the sibling `obli-*` ecosystem exists beyond specification.*
  `obli-transpiler-framework` / `obli-fs` / `obli-riscv-dev-kit` are ROADMAP
  "specification pending"; none was touched here.
